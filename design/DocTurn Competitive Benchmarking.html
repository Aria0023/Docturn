PerfectServe vs. TigerConnect — what to borrow, what to skip.

A side-by-side of the two market-leading clinical communication & collaboration (CC&C) platforms, read through DocTurn's lens: an automated hospitalist patient-assignment engine with secure comms. The capability matrix maps where each vendor is strong; the Adopt / Adapt / Avoid register turns that into a build decision for DocTurn.

01 Vendors at a glance

Two unified platforms; DocTurn is a focused wedge

Both incumbents have spent a decade widening from secure texting into full "care activation" suites — scheduling, alarms, operator consoles, patient engagement. DocTurn deliberately does not compete on breadth. It competes on one job the incumbents leave to humans: deciding which provider receives each new patient.

PerfectServe

Unite · Lightning Bolt

Unified platform spanning clinical communication, provider & staff scheduling, a cloud operator console, a medical answering service, and patient engagement. Signature strength is dynamic intelligent routing to the right on-call person.

Assignment + secure comms

03 The decision register

Adopt · Adapt · Avoid — for DocTurn

The actionable output. Each row is a competitor pattern and DocTurn's recommended stance, with rationale tied to DocTurn's wedge (automated, fair hospitalist assignment) rather than feature parity.

Adopt

Lift these directly

Adopt
Coverage auto-forward
When a provider goes off-shift, redirect their messages to the covering role. DocTurn already models working status + rotation — this is a small, high-value extension.
src: TigerConnect auto-forward; PerfectServe routing
Adopt
Message lifecycle & auto-delete TTL
Read receipts and time-bound message retention are table-stakes for clinical comms and a clean compliance story. Pairs with the existing PHI-log model.
src: TigerConnect message TTL / receipts
Adopt
Mobile PIN / biometric lock
Both incumbents ship device-level PIN locks. Cheap to add to the mobile app, materially improves the security posture on shared devices.
src: TigerConnect PIN locks
Adopt
HITRUST roadmap
HITRUST is the certification enterprise health-system buyers ask for first. Not a feature — a sales unlock. Plan the path even if v1 ships HIPAA-only.
src: TigerConnect HITRUST
Adapt

Borrow the idea, fit our model

Adapt
Schedule-aware routing
PerfectServe's dynamic routing keys off on-call schedules. DocTurn routes off census. Combine: filter the round-robin pool by who is actually scheduled/on-shift before applying lowest-census — fairness and coverage-correctness.
src: PerfectServe dynamic intelligent routing
Adapt
Provider scheduling — lightweight
Full physician scheduling (Lightning Bolt) is a product unto itself. DocTurn needs only enough: defined shifts (Day/Swing/Night) feeding the rotation pool. Resist building a scheduling suite.
src: PerfectServe Lightning Bolt; TigerConnect scheduling
Adapt
EHR / ADT census feed
Incumbents integrate deeply via HL7/FHIR & Epic. DocTurn doesn't need that depth — but a one-way ADT/FHIR feed to auto-update census would remove the system's biggest manual step.
src: both — EHR/FHIR integration
Adapt
AI "care activation"
Both gesture at AI orchestration. DocTurn's AI intake (note→structured fields) is the right seed — extend it toward assignment explanations & anomaly flags, not a generic AI layer.
src: TigerConnect AI orchestration
Avoid

Out of scope — the incumbents' breadth is their cost, not ours

Avoid
Alarm management & nurse call
FDA 510(k)-cleared, hardware-integrated, heavily regulated. Years of scope for a different buyer. Hard no for DocTurn.
Avoid
Operator console / answering service
Call-center replacement (PerfectServe's roots, Five9 partnership). Adjacent market, different sales motion.
Avoid
Patient engagement suite
App-less patient texting, appointment reminders, no-show reduction. Different buyer (ambulatory/marketing), dilutes the wedge.
Avoid
Interfacility transfer & pre-hospital
TigerConnect Transfer is a standalone logistics product. Revisit only if DocTurn expands beyond the inpatient hospitalist floor.
04 Positioning

The wedge: automate the assignment decision

Both incumbents are message-movers — they get the right message to whoever is already designated on-call. Neither decides which hospitalist should own a new admit. That decision is still a charge nurse with a whiteboard. DocTurn owns it.

◎

Win where they're absent

Census-fair, auto-expiring, auto-rerouting assignment is a blank cell in both competitors' matrices. Lead every demo with it.

⤳

Match table-stakes, don't exceed

Secure messaging, MFA, audit, multi-tenant isolation must feel complete — but they're the price of entry, not the pitch. Adopt the small lifecycle/coverage gaps and stop.

⊘

Stay narrow on purpose

Every "Avoid" row is a quarter not spent chasing the incumbents into regulated, hardware-bound, or wrong-buyer territory. Depth on one job beats breadth.

✓
One-line strategy. Be the system of record for who gets the patient — and just enough secure comms around that decision to never send a clinician to a second app.
